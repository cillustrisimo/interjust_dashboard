About the Project

An open-access digital platform that maps universal jurisdiction laws across 216 countries, empowering survivors, lawyers, and investigators to pursue accountability for international crimes beyond borders. InterJust is expanding the platform to include legal frameworks on torture, enforced disappearance, victim and witness protection, the admissibility of digital and open-source evidence, and corporate accountability pathways â€” making it a powerful legal and advocacy tool to advance universal jurisdiction in practice.

Scope/Data Coverage

  • Comparative view of how UN Member States criminalize core international crimes
  • Regional breakdowns and trends
  • Case data showing where national courts have exercised jurisdiction
  • Links to underlying legal frameworks and official sources

Who this is for

  • Policymakers tracking compliance with international norms
  • Researchers analyzing global justice trends
  • Advocates promoting accountability for international crimes
  • Survivors/Victims of crimes
